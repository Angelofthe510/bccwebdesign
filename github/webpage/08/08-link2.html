Star Trek

Star Trek is a sci-fi media franchise, which began with a 1960s TV series and expanded into various films, television series, video games, novels, and comic books with an estimated $10.6 billion in revenue.

Lucille Ball

In early 1964, Desilu studio head Lucille Ball was instrumental in approving production of the series. NBC paid to make the pilot "The Cage" and starred Jeffrey Hunter as Captain Christopher Pike. NBC rejected The Cage, but the executives were still impressed with the concept, and made the unusual decision to commission a second pilot: "Where No Man Has Gone Before".

Gene Roddenberry

Gene was an American television screenwriter, producer, and creator of Star Trek. Born in El Paso, Texas, Roddenberry grew up in Los Angeles, where his father was a police officer. Roddenberry flew in the Army Air Forces during WW2 then a commercial pilot after the war. Later, he joined the LAPD, where he also began to write scripts for TV cop shows.
